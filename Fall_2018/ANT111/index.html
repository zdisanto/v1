This course introduces students to the vast and fascinating sweep of human
biological and cultural evolution. The first half of the course addresses
evolutionary theory and the evidence for human evolution derived from
human genetics and biological diversity, primate behavior, and
paleoanthropology. The second half presents archaeological theories and
methods, and develops a narrative of cultural evolution from the earliest
tool making, to the rise of the great ancient civilizations, to current issues
in prehistory.
Offered: Fall, Spring.
